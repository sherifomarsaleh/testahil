What the chart says

In plain terms: NVIDIA trades a few percent above our weighted central fair value (about $204) — roughly fairly valued, and priced on the durability of the AI data-center build-out rather than on today's earnings alone. It runs a net-cash balance sheet and earns a return on capital employed near 62% against a ~10.5% cost of capital, so the debate is not whether the returns are real but how many years the super-normal phase lasts. The bull case (~$287) is AI-infrastructure spend compounding toward ~$1tn a year while the runway extends and the multiple holds; the cautious case (~$147) is a broad AI-capex digestion or bubble scare with China Data Center still foreclosed by export controls. What moves it is the AI data-center capex path, the China export-control question, and customer concentration among a handful of hyperscalers.

Where NVIDIA sits in its markets

NVIDIA designs the accelerated-computing platform — GPUs, networking and the CUDA software stack — that trains and runs modern AI, and holds roughly four-fifths of the AI-accelerator market. Peers trade in different currencies and aren’t directly value-comparable, so this is a competitive map, not a price table.

The investment debate turns less on any single arena than on how long AI data-center capex compounds and how much of that spend NVIDIA keeps as the merchant hyperscalers design their own silicon and AMD scales the alternative. Competitive map, not advice.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
