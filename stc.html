What the chart says

In plain terms: stc Group trades close to our weighted central fair value of SAR 47.11 — a genuinely converged read across four independent lenses. The primary FCFF DCF lands highest at SAR 50.12 (a five-year forward build discounted at the company's WACC); the dividend discount model (SAR 45.88) and relative EV/EBITDA multiples (SAR 47.21) sit almost exactly on the current price; the through-cycle normalized-earnings floor (SAR 43.29) is a touch below spot. This is Saudi Arabia's dominant integrated telecom operator — near-91%-equity-financed, with net debt/EBITDA near 0.3x — where the swing factor is the dividend-cover math: free cash flow covers the dividend about 0.93x at the base FY26E capex plan (~16.5% of revenue) for the 5G/FTTH build-out, tightening to roughly 0.86x at the top of capex guidance. The bull case (~SAR 59) is that capex intensity eases while CBU ARPU keeps compounding; the bear case (~SAR 36) is that capex stays elevated while competitive intensity caps ARPU growth. What moves it near-term is the SAMA/Fed policy-rate path (the discount-rate channel) and the pace of the 5G/FTTH build.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
