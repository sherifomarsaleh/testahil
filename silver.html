Trend, momentum and key levels — what is the price doing now?

What the chart says

In plain terms: silver pays nothing and has no cash flows, so its value rests primarily on the gold-silver ratio (~64× today: at the long-run average silver is worth ~$65, essentially spot) and the analyst consensus (~$78–$86 for 2026), cross-checked against a sixth-straight structural deficit that has drawn ~800 Moz from stocks since 2021. Fair value NOW is a zone of $58–$78 (centre ~$68), with spot ~$62 in its lower half after a halving from a January all-time high near $121. The near-term cap is rising real rates, a firm dollar and record solar thrifting; the upside is ratio compression and a gold reversion.

The five forces — drag to explore

the most decision-relevant of the 11 drivers

There are two kinds of slider. The first three are steady pressures: the number is how much that force pushes silver each quarter — e.g. −2.5% / quarter means it is right now dragging silver down about 2.5% over three months; slide it positive and it lifts silver instead. The last two are one-off events: the number is the chance it happens and the jump if it does — e.g. 25% · +5% is a 25% chance, worth +5% to silver. Either way, dragging right is better for silver, left is worse.

higher-for-longerFed cuts / easing

What it is: how attractive cash and bonds are next to gold, which pays no interest. While the Fed holds rates high (“higher-for-longer”), money prefers bonds and gold slips — the biggest near-term drag, and the reason for the fall off the January high. Drag right (rate cuts) to ease the drag and lift the forecast; left to deepen it.

dollar surgesdollar weakens

What it is: the US dollar’s strength. Gold is bought and sold in dollars, so a stronger dollar makes it pricier everywhere else and cools demand (DXY is above 100, its highest since May-2025). Drag right (weaker dollar) to push the forecast up; left (dollar surge) to push it down.

gold falls / ratio widensgold rises / ratio compresses

What it is: silver’s single biggest driver — it is dragged by gold through the gold-silver ratio (~64× today). If gold rises toward its own consensus zone and the ratio compresses toward its long-run ~62×, silver is pulled up with no change in its own story. Drag right (gold up / ratio compressing) to lift silver; left (gold down / ratio widening) to soften it. The study sets a mild positive tilt here.

ample stockssqueeze

What it is: silver is a small, thin market with above-ground stocks drawn down for six straight years. A COMEX/LBMA inventory draw or a mine disruption can force a squeeze. This one is an event: the number is the chance of a squeeze this quarter and the +5% it would add. Drag right for a higher chance; left for ample stocks.

calmrisk-off surge

What it is: fear. Wars, crises and market panics send money into precious metals as a safe haven — silver amplifies gold’s haven moves in both directions. This is an event: the number is the chance of a haven shock this quarter and the +5% it would add. Drag right for a higher chance of a risk-off surge; left for calm.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
