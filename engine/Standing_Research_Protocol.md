Standing Research Protocol
Default for every new study.
When I name a target ticker (any exchange) and provide its OHLC price data, produce the full valuation study end-to-end without further instruction, in the order below.
Step 0 — Calibration backtest (before anything else; v3 gate, adopted 10-Jul-2026; GATE BASIS CORRECTED 11-Jul-2026)
Run a 5-year walk-forward backtest on the target ticker, h = 60 trading days, non-overlapping windows: at each origin, using only data up to that date, generate the 3-month-ahead distribution with the v3 engine under the market's own MarketProfile, and score the realized price against it.
DATA-QUALITY GATE FIRST (new 11-Jul-2026, mandatory): no series enters a backtest or a calibration panel until it passes engine/data_quality.py. It drops pre-listing placeholder rows and phantom non-trading rows, and back-adjusts unadjusted corporate actions. Detection is PER-MARKET and derived from each exchange's own daily price limit — a single-session move beyond the limit is not reachable by trading, so it can only be a corporate action or a data error. A single global threshold is WRONG: KOSPI permits ±30%, so an EGX-calibrated threshold would falsely "repair" a legitimate Korean limit-down. This gate is not optional and is not a formality — it has already found an unadjusted corporate action sitting inside a production fit (SODIC/OCDI, a fake −73% crash), pre-IPO placeholder rows reading as a fake +333% jump (EFIH), and ~160 phantom rows per name in the Korean vendor export (144 of Samsung's on a Sunday, KOSPI closed) that had left Korea's published fit simultaneously too narrow and falsely fat-tailed.
Benchmark: the CARRY-ANCHORED lognormal random-walk cone — spot × exp(carry) centre, trailing-252d realized vol width, carry = ln(1+rf) − ln(1+q) from the same MarketProfile schedule the engine uses. Both sides carry the same anchor, so skill measures signal + width, never the time-value of money.
Scores: CRPS skill (primary), pinball-0.5 and Winkler-90 co-reported. PIT and 50/80/90% coverage are diagnostics only; band-containment / hit-rate is NEVER the grade.
THE POOLED SCORE IS SCALE-NORMALIZED (crps ÷ spot, per window, before pooling) — corrected 11-Jul-2026. CRPS is denominated in price units, so summing it raw across a panel weighted each name by its SHARE PRICE, not by its information content: IHC (382 AED) carried 57.9% of the 14-name UAE panel and ELM (874 SAR) carried 58.7% of the 11-name Saudi panel — a "panel verdict" that was arithmetically a one-name verdict. The same distortion applied within a name across time. Normalizing changed NO verdict anywhere, but it tightened the CIs sharply (UAE ±0.07 → ±0.01) and de-inflated the headline skills (Egypt's pooled PASS restates +0.059 → +0.039). The raw price-weighted figure is still reported alongside so previously published numbers stay reconcilable.
Verdict is three-way and pooled: the name's own bootstrap CI gives PASS/PARITY/FAIL as a diagnostic, but the market-panel pooled CI is the standing gate. Proceed if the market panel is PASS or the name is PARITY-or-better. Robust-verdict rule: a name-level FAIL requires the CI entirely below zero across bootstrap block sizes {2,3,4}; a block-dependent sign flip is a BOUNDARY case, recorded PARITY-flagged, never a silent proceed.
BREAK FILTERING (adopted 11-Jul-2026): the calibration sample excludes windows whose origin precedes a market's last declared structural break. Validated, not assumed — on Egypt, calibrating on post-2023-01-11 origins beat calibrating on all windows out-of-sample (LONO +0.0211 vs +0.0198, both scored on the same post-break windows) AND narrowed the cone (cal 0.972 → 0.909). Known gap: MarketProfile.breaks is honoured in the CALIBRATION SAMPLE (panel_refresh.apply_breaks) but mc_v2/mc_v3 still never read it — the engine's per-origin volatility estimation is NOT break-aware. Fixing that would move every published distribution and is an open decision.
ν IS WEAKLY IDENTIFIED — DO NOT QUOTE IT AS PRECISE. Profiling the likelihood across ν on the refreshed panels: every value from 5 to the Gaussian limit sits inside the 95% likelihood interval (UAE); ν=4 through 15 are indistinguishable (Saudi). ν also trades off against width_cal (a fatter tail wants a wider scale to fit the same residuals). The (ν, width_cal) PAIR is what is fitted; neither coordinate is individually precise.
SELECTION PROCEDURE — MLE, and this was tested: selecting (ν, width_cal) by directly maximizing CRPS skill instead of by maximum likelihood looked clearly better in-sample (+0.0038 UAE vs the incumbent's −0.0017) but LOST out-of-sample under leave-one-name-out in both markets tried (UAE +0.0021 vs MLE's +0.0032; Saudi −0.0011 vs +0.0008). It overfits. REJECTED — do not revive without new evidence. MLE-on-pooled-LONO-residuals is retained.

Probability read — §3 opener (presentation layer, no engine change)
§3 opens with a five-row probability-read table computed from the same 50,000 paths the fan chart uses: P(price above spot) at T+60; P(+10 %) vs P(−10 %) with the odds ratio; the median level and its % move; the 50 % band (25th–75th, and as a % of spot); and touch(+10 %) / touch(−10 %). It is presentation-only — a summary of the existing distribution computed after simulation — so it never feeds the drift or the width and leaves CRPS / PIT / Step 0 mathematically unchanged. The direction it shows is only ever what survived Step 0's pooled gate: the carry anchor (an EGP name's median sits ≈ +5 % at T+60 at a ~19.5 % rf before any signal) plus the market's validated signal alpha. Where carry ≈ 0 because the name pays its risk-free carry out as dividends (e.g. a GCC bank with rf ≈ dividend yield), the card says so explicitly — "expected total return ≈ rf, delivered as dividend; price ≈ flat ± band" — an explained flat median, never an unexplained one. Direction is never manufactured. On the site, every ticker page carries the equivalent live card ("The probability read") at the top of its Monte Carlo lab, wired to the page's sliders and horizon toggle (patched phdc.html is the style-parity reference).
Monte Carlo engine — drift, width, shape (v3, "carry-anchored YZ-HAR-t"; adopted 10-Jul-2026)
Used in both Step 0 and §3; lives in engine/mc_v3.py with engine/market_profiles.py (the per-market parameter registry). mc_v2.py is retained as the legacy/ladder reference only. Validation: engine/mc_v3_validation_20260710.md (Egypt pooled L0→L4 ladder).
Drift — carry anchor + shrunk signal, never a raw sample mean:
carry = [ln(1+rf) − ln(1+q)] × h/252, the forward-consistent centre for a PRICE forecast.
signal alpha = IC × σ_h × sign × clip(z, ±2), dead zone |z| < 0.5, hard cap ±0.5·σ_h — Grinold-sized.
EVERY MARKET NOW RUNS CARRY-ONLY (11-Jul-2026). Egypt's rev_1m signal was the LAST active signal in the system and was ABLATED OFF on evidence: on the 27-name panel its empirical IC is +0.018 — the house prior's contrarian sign=−1 is REFUTED and the magnitude is ~zero — and carry-only beats signal-on out-of-sample (+0.0252 vs +0.0211), helping in only 13 of 25 names, with a paired bootstrap P(signal helps) = 0.31. India's mom_12_1 shows the same wrong-sign pattern (IC −0.093 against a +1 prior). The signal machinery is retained and the per-market priors are kept for re-estimation as panels grow, but no market's panel currently supports an active signal. A market whose pooled panel cannot establish its signal runs carry-only (fallback rule) — that is now all of them.
The §1 fundamental value gap NEVER enters the drift (lens independence). The raw expanding-window secular mean drift is RETIRED (do-not-revive).
Width — the pooled log-HAR cascade (variance lags 1/5/22) on the gap-aware Yang-Zhang proxy, as v2, PLUS: the lognormal half-variance bias correction exp(s²/2) on the log-regression forecast, a 0.8/0.2 log-space shrink toward the trailing-252d proxy mean (estimation-noise control), and a per-market width_cal multiplier from the LONO shape fit. Regime-conditional by construction. The ×1.15/×1.30 kvol floor stays retired.
Shape — unit-variance Student-t(ν) via the per-path chi-square mixture; ν is FITTED per market on pooled standardized 60-day residuals, leave-one-name-out cross-fitted — never hard-coded. A market without a panel borrows the archetype fit, width_cal = 1, FLAGGED, and a borrowed config may never ground a FAIL.

Step 2 — Information Sweep & Financials (sweep adopted 10-Jul-2026)
2A. The Information Sweep — exhaustive, ring-by-ring, before any forecast driver is set.
The roll-forward's blind spot is the discontinuity: a base-resetting one-off (the PHDC / Ras-El-Hekma class), a business-model break (the LLM-vs-SaaS class), or newly disclosed granularity each change either the base being grown or the method allowed — and none are visible in last year's margins. So every study opens its fundamental work with a structured sweep of the ticker's world, and the sweep's findings are stated in the report. Coverage is measured by categories closed, never query count; a category searched with nothing found is closed by a dated negative search — silence never closes anything.
Rings — equity (outside-in, all four mandatory):
(1) Global — the rate cycle and USD/FX regime as they bind the name; the commodity complex where an input or output; global sector demand; trade / sanctions / supply-chain shifts.
(2) Country — sovereign macro (inflation, policy rate, FX / devaluation risk, program status); the regulatory environment binding this name (sector regulator, price caps, tariffs, tax / subsidy changes, capital controls); fiscal / political events with sector read-through.
(3) Industry — demand drivers and the capacity / supply balance; pricing; competitive structure at the named-competitor level: new entrants, technology substitution, competitor capacity adds and price moves. The Microsoft-vs-LLM class of risk lives in this ring, not the company ring.
(4) Company (the decisive ring) — strategic plans and guidance; regular disclosures; investor-relations communications (calls, presentations, releases); news, with one-off transactions that reset the revenue / asset / backlog base as an explicit search target; ownership / stake changes via the standing named-transaction rule; management and capital actions; the official financial statements themselves.
Rings — metals (all four mandatory): (1) Global macro & policy — real rates, USD, the central-bank path, official-sector behavior; (2) Supply — mine production, recycling, disruptions; (3) Demand — industrial, jewelry, investment / ETF flows, official-sector purchases; (4) Market structure — positioning (COT), the forward curve / lease rates, regulatory treatment (e.g. Basel III). Primary / official sources preferred throughout (WGC, USGS, LBMA, CFTC, central banks).
Provenance (hard rule): financial-statement line items come only from the company's official financial statements — audited FS, annual reports, company IR releases, or the exchange's official disclosure portal as the filing channel. An aggregator is never a source for a financial-statement figure; aggregators remain acceptable for market data (prices, yields, FX) only.
Classification — every finding takes one of four classes, each with a stated modeling consequence:
B (base-changer) — resets the starting point of the roll-forward. Modeled as an explicit, dated event, never smoothed into a growth glide, and the headline shown in both framings (with / without the event) per the standing dual-framing rule.
S (structural / assumption-breaker) — breaks a forward driver assumption (a new entrant, a substitution, a regime change). The affected driver is re-set or shown as a sensitized range, with the finding cited.
D (driver-unlock) — newly disclosed granularity (volumes, ASP, unit economics, backlog) that converts a top-down line to a bottom-up build; triggers re-evaluation of the data-discipline gate for that driver.
C (color) — context only: logged, capped, changes no number.
Stated in the report: the findings appear narratively where they already belong (§1.8 macro, Appendix B industry / peers, the company sections), and Appendix B closes with the consolidated Sweep Register — ring · category · class · finding · source · date — negative searches included. §1.6 opens with the per-driver gate table: each major driver, its mode (bottom-up / top-down) and the sweep line that justified it. Bottom-up is the default wherever the sweep cleared it; top-down without a completed sweep is a QC fail, and forcing bottom-up without the disclosure is equally a fail.
Cadence & freshness: the full sweep runs at every study and every scheduled (3-month) update; if more than 10 trading days pass between the sweep and delivery, the company ring re-runs.
Ledger interaction: a driver later shown to have been disclosed at sweep time but missed closes in the Fundamental Driver Ledger as a sourcing failure (SF) — distinct from a genuine gut-check (GC) — and an SF row triggers a named fix to the sweep's search pattern, not just an annotation.
Enforcement: the sweep is recorded in engine/research_sweep.py (SweepRegister). validate() fails the build if any mandatory category is unclosed, any financial-statement figure carries a non-official source, any B / S / D finding names no model impact, or the driver gate table is missing / uncited. The register JSON ships with the study files; its verdict is QC item (m).
2B. Financials.
Pull the company's historical financial data from its official financial statements only (audited FS, annual reports, company IR releases; the exchange disclosure portal counts as the official filing channel). Record the source and reporting / as-of date for every figure used. Aggregators are never a source for a financial-statement line item (2A provenance rule); they remain acceptable for market data only.
Step 3 — Methodology (lens selection)
Select the instrument-appropriate lens yourself and flag the lens choice plus any judgment calls after the draft:
RE developer / land-monetisation → SOTP / RNAV
Pure recurring-income RE operator (malls / hotels, no meaningful development pipeline) → 10-year DCF with an explicit terminal cap rate
Mixed RE name (development + recurring, e.g. ORHD) → split the legs: development = RNAV, recurring / hospitality = a cap-rate / DCF sleeve — never one blended RNAV
Contractor → going-concern FCFF + SOTP (contractor driver tree — USD-primary DCF off a contract-award schedule, stubs valued separately — in §3.5-F)
Bank → DDM
Holding company → holdco NAV (disciplined-SOTP mechanics in §3.5-F)
Aggregator / digital-distribution platform, or payments / recurring-licence software → DCF, with the class driver tree in §3.5-F (take-rate migration, or revenue-quality mix + organic-vs-FX)
Operating company with a captive finance arm (e.g. GB Corp) → split the legs: operating arm = DCF, captive lender = equity book × a return-justified multiple, a fintech / finance associate marked to its last funding round — never one blended entity (§3.5-F)
The RE lens split and the split-NAV architecture are detailed in §3.5-E.
Step 3.5 — §1 fundamental: driver-build & transparency rules
EFG-derived; apply in §1.6 / §1.7 and enforce at the QC gate. Derived from benchmarking Testahil studies against EFG Hermes coverage — telecom (du / STC), staples (Almarai / Savola), chemicals (Sidpec / Advanced), the EFID round (Edita / CIB / ADIB / HELI / EGAL), a dedicated RE-developer round (§3.5-E), and a same-name / sector-proxy round matching nine of our names (ORWE, ORAS, FWRY, EFIH, RAYA, OIH, BTFH, HRHO, CCAP) against EFG's Oriental Weavers, Orascom Construction, HPS, Rasan, EK Holding and GB Corp (new instrument classes in §3.5-F). A. Portable devices
Apply on every study, on top-down normalized-margin forecasts; devices 1–6 require no disclosed volume / ASP. Devices 7–8 are transparency rules (generalized out of the RE overlay from the HPS two-vintage read) and apply to any high-terminal or multi-method valuation.
Swing in real units. Sensitize the crux on its observable driver in native units — urea $/t + gas $/mmbtu (fertilizer), product–feedstock spread $/t (petchem), LME / alumina $/t (smelter), the EGP rate, a tariff-step %, the CBE rate path — never an abstract EBITDA-margin %. Those are market quotes, not guesses. (EFG-Advanced's PP-naphtha spread table is the template.)
DPS schedule + stress check. For yield names, an explicit DPS / payout schedule and a sustainability test under the bear / crux-shock scenario.
Forecast-vs-actual scorecard. On every 3-month update, a table scoring the prior study's point estimates (revenue / EBITDA / EPS) against the actual print, line by line — the fundamental analogue of the Step 0 ledger. (Working template: HPS Fig 24, a full 1H23a-vs-1H23e P&L with Y-o-Y / H-o-H / Vs-Est columns. Live instance: EFG's Apr-2025 ORWE report put 2025 EPS at 4.65 vs an actual 3.33 — a 28 % miss — a concrete case of EFG's own optimism missing.)
New-vs-old reconciliation. On updates, old vs new estimate per line, with the driver of each change named.
Standardized ratio dashboard. A fixed per-name panel — EPS / DPS / BVPS, P/E, P/B, FCF yield, div yield, EV/EBITDA, EV/sub (or sector metric), ROAIC, ROAE, margins, effective tax, net-debt/EBITDA — identical across every study (EFG's "Data Miner" page). This is the Per-Share & Ratios block. (Cost half = an opex-%-of-revenue decomposition, per HPS Fig 17.)
Peak / mid-cycle framing. State where current earnings sit in the cycle (peak / trough / mid) before normalizing.
Terminal-value dependency disclosure. For any high-terminal DCF (not just income-RE per §3.5-E), disclose TV as a % of total EV and sensitize on it. HPS's 2022 DCF was ~93 % terminal value (PV-of-TV 7,479 of EV 8,004) — a terminal-g-vs-Ke bet dressed as a 5-year cash-flow model. Make that dependency visible rather than burying it.
Never blend a DCF and a multiple that are far apart into one point. EFG averages a DCF value (HPS MAD11,485) with a peer-multiple value (MAD4,076) ~2.5–2.8× apart into a single TP (7,780; and 934 / 380 → 657 in '23). A 50 / 50 blend of values that divergent is arbitrary false precision — the football-field-plus-distribution is the correct replacement (and reinforces the no-rating rule).
B. Class-conditional §1.6 driver builds
Use the class's driver tree only when the data-discipline gate (C) is passed.
Consumer / staples → packs × price-per-pack with explicit price-tier / price-point migration.
Banks → rate-path-driven NIM bridge + DuPont RoAA decomposition + forward CET1 / CAR capital schedule (RWAs, density, CET1 vs management floors); for a zero-dividend serial capital-raiser the capital trajectory is the dividend-initiation thesis.
RE developer / land-monetisation → disclosed contract-minimum / instalment-collection schedules. (Full RE overlay — split-NAV, escrow-DPS engine, discount band, cap-rate discipline — in §3.5-E.)
Commodity / smelter → an explicit LME / alumina (or relevant commodity) price deck with input costs modelled separately.
Petchem / fertilizer → the product–feedstock spread built in $/t and $/mmbtu.
Aggregator / digital-distribution platform, payments / recurring-licence software, contractor, holding company, operating-co + captive lender → see §3.5-F for each class's driver tree.
C. Data-discipline gate
Overrides any "build bottom-up" impulse.
Default to top-down normalized margins on disclosed averages. Build a bottom-up driver model only when every driver is separately disclosed by the company or is an observable market quote; if bottom-up, show the sourced driver table first so the base is visibly real, and fall back to top-down for any driver that can't be sourced. Never manufacture volume / ASP splits — false precision (two multiplied guesses) can be worse than an honest top-down average.
Before assigning any forward-looking driver not sourced from disclosure or a market quote (a "gut check"), check the Fundamental Driver Ledger for a same-class prior. If 2 of the last 3 closed instances of that driver-class missed in the same direction by a material margin, do not reuse the heuristic unmodified — correct for the known bias or present the driver as a sensitized range rather than a single point.
For any driver representing a company's ownership stake, equity interest, or similar arm's-length position in another entity (an associate, a subsidiary stake, a JV interest), search specifically for the named transaction (an acquisition, a capital raise, a disposal) that most recently changed that position — stake percentages are frequently disclosed only in one-off deal press releases, not in recurring financial statements. Never mark such a driver "estimated, not separately published" without first running this as a dedicated search, separate from the general earnings-release research pass.
Replicability without the EFG report: these builds are reconstructable from primary sources (filings, EGX disclosures, CBE corridor data), but forward commodity / price-path inputs are the house's own defensible view, and a few inputs (bank stage 1/2/3 coverage and exact RWA-density path; smelter cost-bridge weights; a contractor's forward award assumptions; a bank stage-mix path) stay flagged estimates, not figures guaranteed to tie to EFG's.
Cleared the bottom-up bar so far: ABUK (urea / ammonia FOB, Egypt gas-feedstock price, tonnage all public); Edita (packs × price from disclosed per-segment volume / price history); ORWE (sqm volume + avg EGP/sqm price disclosed split local vs export, per EFG Fig 7 — the household-durables packs × price build, with the export rebate modelled as an explicit declining %-of-export-sales line as the 70 % subsidy cut phases in); Rasan (GWP + per-segment take-rate both disclosed).
Clears a backlog-driven revenue build (not a full bottom-up): ORAS (disclosed backlog clears a book-to-bill revenue conversion, but the forward contract-award schedule stays the house's own flagged view).
Stay top-down: Juhayna (per-litre ASP undisclosed); ETEL (subs × ARPU undisclosed). For FWRY / EFIH, verify the take-rate and recurring-mix disclosure before building bottom-up, else stay top-down.
D. Discipline
Adopt EFG's machinery (the driver tree), not their more-optimistic top-line / terminal assumptions — their bullish numbers (e.g. Edita 22 % CAGR / EGP38.5 TP; ORWE's 2025 EPS 4.65 that printed 3.33) sit behind undisclosed terminal assumptions or plain optimism; driver-tree conservatism defended by sourced drivers beats chasing their optimism. Never copy EFG's rating / target-price / upside apparatus or promotional register ("we called it before") — Testahil publishes fair-value ranges and probability distributions, never a rating.
E. RE-developer / property overlay
Sits on top of A–D. Current standard anchors: EMFD / HELI / OCDI / ORHD (TMGH / PHDC are legacy-density comparison points). Derived from batch-comparing our RE studies against EFG Mai-Attia RE coverage: TMG, RAK, Aldar, MCDC, Emaar Development, Mabanee.
Split-NAV architecture (house rule). Development legs carry no terminal value (finite, sold over 10–15 yrs; Aldar states this for its project-management leg); only recurring / investment legs get perpetual cap-rate treatment. This keeps the two clocks (fundamental value vs full-execution ceiling) from blurring inside the NAV.
Six RE mechanics (beyond A's devices):
Escrow / handover cash-release DPS engine (Emaar Dev = gold standard). DPS built project-by-project off the handover schedule (units handed-over × margin → cash release → payout %), not a % of reported net income (≠ cash under completed-contract recognition). For EGX use collection-schedule-based (no RERA escrow regime); clears the data gate for names disclosing collection / handover schedules (PHDC, TMGH, EMFD). The deep form of device A-2.
NAV-discount band anchored to the name's own history (TMG 40 % vs 7-yr avg 45 %; Emaar Dev 23 % vs 8-yr avg 32 %). The RNAV multiple carries a stated discount / premium vs the stock's own historical trading-discount-to-NAV, not an arbitrary haircut.
Terminal-cap-rate discipline. When DCF-ing an income-RE name the exit cap rate is the valuation: disclose TV as % of total (MCDC TV = 74 %) and sensitize on the cap rate. The income-RE analogue of "swing in real units." (The general form is device A-7.)
Cap-rate / NOI-yield discipline on recurring segments — per-segment NOI yield + EBITDA-margin trend + WAULT + yield-compression narrative + tenant concentration (Aldar template). Confirmed shallow in our current set.
New-asset ramp / stabilization curve — model occupancy ramp explicitly for a big pipeline asset (Mabanee 25 → 50 → 70 → 85 % over 4 yrs); never assume instant stabilization.
Binary-event catastrophic scenario in §1.7 — quantify the single-point-dependency case (MCDC no-Hajj → revenue −40 %, NI −23 %) for names hinged on one mega-project / season / regulatory event.
Property-cycle framing replaces "normalized earnings power" (§1.4) for developers — each project is finite, so normalized EPS is near-meaningless. Instead state cycle position: Dubai-2020 analogy, off-plan-penetration curve, months into the uptrend. This is the defensible form of the near-absent normalized-earnings gap.
RE optimism filter — take the machinery, not the discount / target. Aldar reconciles up to management's aspirational 2030 target; MCDC's 5 % terminal cap with TV = 74 % is aggressive (stress a higher cap); Mabanee's 24.5 % CAGR rides unopened assets (make the ramp the crux). Exception worth copying on the number (not just the machinery): Emaar Dev models cyclicality down — mid-cycle margin compression, extended payment terms from 2027, ~33 % sales decline into the trough.
RE data-discipline. Presales → revenue waterfall and collection-schedule DPS clear the bar for EGX developers (instalment / collection schedules disclosed); NOI-yield builds clear for names with malls / hotels; manufactured sqm × price splits stay barred.

F3. Contractor (EFG Orascom Construction → ORAS). Lens = going-concern FCFF + SOTP. (d) A SOTP-per-share table (EV / equity / holdco-share / per-share / % contribution columns) = the holdco Data Miner. Keeps the two clocks inside the NAV.

G. Cost-of-equity (Ke) and WACC — bottom-up method, adapted by market type
Never use a flat house ERP, an assumed beta, or a copy-pasted rate across markets — the method is standing, the inputs are never generic. Check Cost_of_Capital_Reference.md first (which market-type guidance applies), then build:
rf — the target market's own local-currency government bond yield, quoted directly from a live market source, when a liquid local market exists. For mature markets (US, W. Europe, Japan), this is just the local govt bond yield, full stop. For GCC/pegged currencies (SAR, AED, QAR), source the real local bond/sukuk yield anyway — the peg does not make it equivalent to UST; this is a documented, named mistake in professional discount-rate practice, not a theoretical concern. For other floating EM currencies, use Damodaran's sovereign-dollar-bond-spread or CDS-spread fallback if no reliable local bond quote exists. (The MC engine's carry anchor draws on the same sourced schedule — one rf discipline, two consumers.)
ERP — from Damodaran's original file only — never a secondary source's summary (a secondary source's claimed 14.87% for Egypt did not match his actual published 13.94%/9.41% and shipped in a study before being caught). Mature markets use his mature-market ERP with no country premium layer. Every other market uses his rating-based figure as primary ("standard practice") and CDS-based as the named "more current" alternative, where a CDS spread exists.
Beta — attempt a genuine regression at the best frequency obtainable, run through RegressionBetaAttempt.is_usable() (n≥24, R²≥5%, SE(beta)<|beta|, beta>0). For mature-market mega-caps this should usually succeed — don't reach for the beta=1.0 fallback just because it's convenient; that fallback is a last resort for genuinely inaccessible data, not a standing shortcut for liquid names with decades of clean daily data. If it fails the gate or the data can't be obtained, default to beta = 1.0 and never substitute a foreign sector-peer beta.
Kd — source the company's actual debt-currency mix via named-transaction search (search "[company] [instrument] EGP/USD facility/bond/loan" as a dedicated query). Price each tranche off real local lending-rate data — for GCC names, a real local rate, never a USD proxy, same peg caveat as rf.
Weights — market cap vs. disclosed total debt, always computed, never assumed.
Metals — this entire method is inapplicable. Gold/Silver are valued via the real-rates/flows/official-sector lens (Expert Persona Library casting), not a WACC-discounted DCF.
Arithmetic — use wacc_builder.py — every input as a sourced WaccInputs field; it raises rather than defaulting if anything is missing.
Publish both ERP-basis WACCs where both exist, and a beta sensitivity grid whenever beta was assumed rather than reliably regressed.

STEP 6 — CONTINUOUS LEARNING (the unattended loop; adopted 11-Jul-2026)
The calibration loop is no longer a standing intention enforced by discipline — it is a real pipeline. This section governs it.

THE OHLC LIBRARY. engine/raw_ohlc/{MARKET}/{TICKER}.csv is a PERSISTENT LIBRARY of every covered stock's price history — not an inbox of "this session's uploads". Adding or refreshing ONE stock means adding or overwriting ONE file. The pipeline then refits that stock's WHOLE market against the complete library. One-stock-at-a-time is the DEFAULT, cheapest path, because that is the natural rhythm of the work (studies are produced one name at a time). Cost of a one-stock post: ~12 seconds even for Egypt's 27 names.
FILE PLACEMENT IS THE TICKER DECISION. The pipeline never infers a ticker from a filename. Disambiguation (ADNOC Gas vs ADNOC Drilling; Abu Dhabi Islamic Bank's EGX-listed subsidiary vs its UAE parent) is a human judgment and stays one.

HOW IT RUNS. A push to engine/raw_ohlc/ triggers a GitHub Actions workflow (also runnable manually, and nightly). It cleans, rebuilds panels, refits, and issues LONO verdicts — then takes exactly one of two paths.
  AUTO-APPLIED, no approval: cleaning, panel rebuild, the (ν, width_cal) refit, per-name and market verdicts — PROVIDED nothing a reader can see would change.
  STOPS AND OPENS A REVIEW PR, never auto-merged, if ANY of:
    · an EXISTING name's verdict category changes;
    · a NEW name arrives already FAILING (the signature of a misfiled or corrupt file);
    · the market-level panel verdict changes;
    · a panel carries a name with no raw data behind it (stale residuals);
    · THE PUBLISHED 90% CONE MOVES MORE THAN 5%.
A NEW NAME IS NOT MATERIAL BY ITSELF. Adding a stock is the most common event in the system; blocking on it would mean a review on every post. Placing the file IS the human decision. New names are always named explicitly in the run output, so an arrival is never invisible.
THE CONE RULE, AND WHY IT IS MEASURED ON THE CONE. Materiality is measured on the band the reader actually sees — width_cal × q95(t(ν)) — NOT on ν and width_cal separately. Those two trade off against each other, so watching them individually both misses real changes and fires on noise. Concretely: cleaning 8 stale gold rows moved the MLE from ν=12 to Gaussian, which a naive "ν regime change" rule called material — but the two are statistically indistinguishable (ΔlogL 0.31) and the published cone moves 0.3%. The cone rule ignores that, while still catching every real change of 11-Jul (Saudi +15.6%, Korea +11.8%, Egypt +5.8%).

WHY IT IS GATED AND NOT MERELY AUTOMATED. On 11-Jul-2026, DATA CLEANING ALONE flipped Korea's tail shape (ν=6 → Gaussian) and changed two names' robust verdicts (SA/RAJHI PARITY→PASS; AE/ALPHADHABI PARITY→FAIL). A pipeline that simply "ran the refit on a schedule" would have pushed both into production with nobody looking. Automation without a materiality gate is not continuous improvement — it is unsupervised drift.

THE PROMOTION RULE (governs the METHOD, not just the data). Nothing enters the engine — proposed by a human or produced by the pipeline — without surviving the same out-of-sample test the forecasts themselves must survive. An idea that improves the score on the data it was selected with has demonstrated NOTHING. Precedent, and it is binding: a proposal to select (ν, width_cal) by maximizing CRPS skill rather than by MLE looked clearly better in-sample and LOST under LONO in both markets tested. It was rejected. Test first, adopt second, in that order, always.

SINGLE SOURCE OF TRUTH. engine/market_profiles.py is the canonical registry that production reads. engine/fitted_configs.json is a DERIVED MIRROR only — never hand-edit it. A session on 11-Jul-2026 briefly wrote fits to the mirror alone; they were read by nothing, and the loop did not actually close until panel_refresh.py wrote back to market_profiles.py. If a fit is not in market_profiles.py, it is not in production.

EXACTNESS OF THE FAST PATH. panel_refresh.fast_rescore re-scores a prebuilt panel under any (ν, width_cal) without re-running the O(n²) HAR fit — legitimate because ν and width_cal enter ONLY at the simulation step (σ_h scales linearly in width_cal; α scales linearly in σ_h; carry is invariant). It was verified BIT-FOR-BIT against backtest_v3 across configurations and both signal states before adoption. A silent fork of the engine is the one failure a self-updating system cannot survive, so any future shortcut must clear the same bar: exact, or not adopted.
