What the chart says

In plain terms: Ibnsina Pharma trades about 52% below our weighted central fair value — cheap on fundamentals, but priced for genuine Egyptian risk. A discounted-cash-flow on the distribution business lands near EGP 19.8 (the most optimistic lens, because it capitalises the margin-and-scale story into a terminal value), relative EV/EBITDA multiples near EGP 16.7 (the most grounded, anchored on what the market pays today for EBITDA), a normalized-earnings read near EGP 18.0, and a dividend-yield floor near EGP 11.0 — an EGP 11.0–19.8 cluster centred on ~EGP 17.8. Ibnsina Pharma is Egypt’s #1–2 pharmaceutical distributor: more than 35,000 customers, ~24% market share (IQVIA), revenue of EGP 76.6bn in FY25 at an ~8% gross and ~5% EBITDA margin, a ~1.2% net margin, and a near-zero cash-conversion cycle so growth is funded by suppliers rather than equity. The bull case (~EGP 22.7) is the net margin normalising as CBE rates ease and the drug-re-pricing cycle feeds through to margins; the cautious case (~EGP 12.9) is a razor-thin margin staying pinned by high finance costs and receivables risk. What moves it near-term is the CBE rate path, drug-re-pricing decisions, and each quarter’s margin print. One caveat specific to this name: in the calibration back-test the Monte-Carlo engine did not beat a simple random walk, so the 1–3-month price map below is illustrative of the method, not a reliable forecast for ISPH.

What drives the odds

Peer set & risks

Where Ibnsina Pharma sits in its market

Ibnsina Pharma is Egypt’s #1–2 pharmaceutical distributor — more than 35,000 customers and ~24% market share, distributing drugs, OTC, cosmetics and medical supplies from a national warehouse-and-delivery network. Its cleanest comparators are the other Egyptian distributors (largely private) and EM healthcare distributors; high local interest rates compress the multiples the market pays relative to global peers. This is a competitive map, not a price table.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
