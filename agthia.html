What the chart says

In plain terms: Agthia trades about 25% below our weighted central fair value — cheap on every fundamental lens, though the tape is soft and sits below all four moving averages. It is Abu Dhabi’s food-security champion: Al Ain water, Grand Mills flour and Agrivita feed, an Egypt-centred snacking portfolio (Abu Auf, BMB, Al Foah) and a protein arm (Nabil, Atyab) ramping a new Saudi plant, with ADQ as the majority owner. Reported FY2025 looked dreadful — EPS 0.103, EBITDA −32% — but AED 143mn of that is ring-fenced provisions, not lost operating power: underlying EBITDA held a 12.5% margin and Q1 2026 already turned (revenue +3.3%, profit +12.5%) while paying a 6.3% dividend yield. On the lenses the consolidated DCF reads AED 4.60, the segment sum-of-the-parts 4.24, conservative multiples 3.83 and normalized earnings 4.51 — a weighted central near 4.37. The bull case is the Snacking margin rebuilding (green coffee and the Egyptian pound are the levers) and the KSA plant filling; the cautious case (~3.05) is a third year of provisions or an Egypt shock. One honesty note: on this name the three-month Monte Carlo ties — matches — a naïve benchmark (its skill margin sits on zero), so the odds below are an honest, well-calibrated probability map rather than a demonstrated edge — the fundamental work is unaffected.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
