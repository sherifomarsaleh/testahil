What the chart says

In plain terms: Orascom Construction trades at EGP 720 against a five-lens weighted fair value near EGP 928 — roughly +29% — but the upside is conditional, not automatic. Two things, not the discount rate, decide whether it is cheap: how much of the reported ~$540m net cash is genuinely distributable rather than client advances and bonding (we haircut it to ~$400m), and whether the OCI Global combination completes. The bull (full net cash credited and the parts re-rated) reaches ~EGP 1,272; the conservative through-cycle earnings lens anchors the low end near ~EGP 740. The backlog is over five times the market cap and the US arm is the fastest-growing piece — but the chart has run eight-fold and is consolidating, so this is a balance-sheet-quality and deal-completion story, not a momentum one.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
