What the chart says

In plain terms: Riyad Bank trades meaningfully below our weighted central fair value of SAR 26.61 — the gap is what its retained capital is worth. On the dividend it pays today (~42% of earnings) the shares are worth about SAR 23.6; on the value it builds by reinvesting a ~16% return on equity, the excess-return lenses land at SAR 32.2–33.2. It is Saudi Arabia's third-largest bank by assets — a low-rate, low-cost-deposit franchise earning ~16% on equity against a ~10.3% cost of equity, at a sub-30% cost-to-income ratio. The bull case (~SAR 33) is that the mid-teens ROE persists through the SAMA easing cycle; the cautious case (~SAR 21) is that the margin fades and the ROE settles closer to the cost of equity, which is roughly what the market's ~1.2× book already prices in. What moves it near-term is the SAMA/Fed rate path and the quarterly NIM print.

Where Riyad Bank sits in its markets

Riyad Bank is Saudi Arabia’s third-largest bank by assets — a mature, low-rate GCC franchise earning ~16% on equity, well above the ~1.2× book the market pays for it. It screens cheaply against Saudi and GCC peers relative to its return. This is a competitive map, not a price table.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
