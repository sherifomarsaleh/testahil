Trend, momentum and key levels — what is the price doing now?

What the chart says

In plain terms: ADIB is the UAE’s most profitable bank — a 28.8% return on equity built on deposits that are 91% current-and-savings — and it is priced for it: 3.31× book against a sector at 1.55×. Five lenses put fair value at AED 17.9–23.5, with a weighted central of AED 21.23 — about 2.4% below the price. On the payout it has held for five straight years (48.5–49.9% of profit), the dividend model alone says AED 22.5; give it credit for the surplus capital a slowing growth rate frees up and the FCFE ceiling is AED 23.5. The crux is durability: at our cost of equity the price already implies a 26% return on equity forever, better than management’s own 25% 2030 target. The bull case (~AED 23) is that the moat is structural and holds; the conservative case (~AED 18) is what the sector actually pays for this return today. What moves it near-term is whether the Strait of Hormuz stays open and how the UAE’s new minimum tax lands.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
