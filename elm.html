What the chart says

In plain terms: Elm Company has crashed ~49% from its January-2025 peak (SAR 1,289, P/E ~56×) to SAR 658.5 (P/E ~24×) — the de-rate has done most of the work of correcting the euphoria, and the shares now trade close to, and slightly above, our weighted central fair value of SAR 620. On a neutral-beta discounted-cash-flow (β 1.0, WACC 10.5%) the business is worth about SAR 576; crediting the ~88% return on invested capital and ~70% recurring revenue, the multiple-based lenses land at SAR 590–690. The whole gap is a discount-rate argument, because ~77% of DCF value is terminal: if the market prices Elm as the low-beta government compounder it has historically been (β ~0.7), fair value is closer to SAR 750; if it re-rates Elm as a high-beta growth name after the crash (β ~1.6), fair value falls toward SAR 400. Elm’s moat is exclusive access to Saudi Arabia’s national data registry behind its highest-margin Digital Business line (73.8% of 2024 revenue); any erosion of that exclusivity is the other swing factor. What moves it near-term is the market’s view of Elm’s risk and any registry-access news — there is no dividend cushion worth mentioning (yield ~1.3%) while the argument resolves.

Where Elm Company sits in its markets

Elm has no clean listed peer on the Tadawul — the closest comparators are global IT-services/gov-tech names and regional digital-platform businesses, though none share Elm’s national-registry exclusivity. On recurring mix, ROIC and margin, Elm screens at or above the better global software-services franchises, which is the basis for its historical premium multiple. How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
