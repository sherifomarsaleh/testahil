What the chart says

In plain terms: The Saudi National Bank trades about 16% below our weighted central fair value — modestly cheap, and unusually, four independent bank lenses converge on the same answer. A two-stage dividend model lands near SAR 44, a discounted-cash-flow (FCFF) build near SAR 45, relative multiples near SAR 46 and a sustainable-return justified price-to-book near SAR 47 — a tight SAR 44–47 cluster. SNB is the Kingdom’s national banking champion, formed from the 2021 NCB–Samba merger: ~SAR 1.26 tn of assets, the deepest low-cost (CASA) deposit base among covered banks, a 74%-fixed-rate investment book that protects gross yield as rates fall, and a 16%+ return on tangible equity at a sector-low cost-to-income. The bull case (~SAR 55) is durable margin resilience and a step-up in payout; the cautious case (~SAR 36) is a faster SAMA cutting cycle that squeezes the net interest margin and a persistent Türkiye / legacy international drag. What moves it near-term is the rate path and the quarterly NIM print.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
