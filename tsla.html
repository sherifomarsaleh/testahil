What the chart says

In plain terms: Tesla trades far above our weighted central fair value — richly valued on the operating businesses, with almost the entire premium resting on the autonomy bet. It is best read as an operating company — a profitable EV maker, a fast-growing energy-storage business, and a small services arm — wrapped around a large option on autonomy and robotics (Full Self-Driving, Robotaxi and Optimus) whose cash flows do not yet exist. On operating cash flows and earnings the business is worth roughly USD 90–230 a share; the bull case (~$560, the top of the sum-of-the-parts) needs Robotaxi and Optimus to scale into large, high-margin businesses, while the cautious case (~$103) is autonomy stalling as the multiple de-rates. What moves it is how likely, how large and how soon the autonomy businesses become real — an outcome the market observes only through the price.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
