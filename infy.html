What the chart says

In plain terms: Infosys trades well below our weighted central fair value — a quality compounder de-rated to a value multiple, but in a clear downtrend near its 52-week low. It is a single, focused business: a global IT-services and consulting franchise that is net-cash, converts more than 100% of profit to cash, earns a ~33% return on equity, and returned over ₹37,500 cr to owners in FY26. The market is pricing one real risk — that generative AI compresses the labour-arbitrage pyramid the whole industry is built on. The bull case (~₹1,556) is that Infosys cannibalises its own hours and keeps the margin while the discretionary-demand cycle recovers and the multiple re-rates; the cautious case (~₹995) is AI deflating pricing faster than it cuts cost. What moves it is where the GenAI-affected margin settles, not the balance sheet.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
