What the chart says

In plain terms: Saudi Basic Industries trades a few percent below our weighted central fair value — roughly fairly valued to modestly cheap, at a cyclical trough in the global petrochemical margin. It is the Gulf's petrochemical champion, ~70%-owned by Saudi Aramco, converting advantaged Saudi gas and naphtha into polymers, agri-nutrients and specialties. 2025 was a genuine trough — Chinese oversupply, weak global demand and a compressed product–feedstock spread pushed the adjusted EBITDA margin to ~15% and produced a large one-off, non-cash writedown on the European assets being divested. The bull case (~SAR 66) is the spread normalising toward a mid-cycle ~19% margin while the low-cost feedstock advantage is credited in full; the cautious case (~SAR 43) is a structurally lower spread — a permanent Chinese-oversupply regime. What moves it is the product–feedstock spread in $/tonne and the timing of the margin-cycle recovery.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
