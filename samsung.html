What the chart says

In plain terms: Samsung trades a little above our weighted central fair value, so the market is already paying for much of the AI-memory boom. The bull case — a durable supercycle, HBM4 wins and disciplined capacity — supports a path toward the ~KRW 411,000 upside scenario; the cautious case, anchored on after-capex owner cash flow and a normalized through-cycle margin, sits nearer ~KRW 215,000. What separates them is almost entirely memory: the direction of DRAM/NAND pricing and whether Samsung closes its high-bandwidth-memory gap with SK Hynix. The balance sheet is a real cushion — net cash of roughly KRW 119 trillion — but it does not protect the earnings, which are ~94% memory and therefore deeply cyclical.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
