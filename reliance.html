What the chart says

In plain terms: Reliance trades modestly below our weighted central fair value — roughly fairly valued, with the swing resting on crystallising the value of its unlisted digital and retail arms. It is best read as a sum of very different businesses — Jio (the largest telecom-and-digital pool), Reliance Retail, the O2C refining-and-petrochemicals complex, upstream oil & gas, media and an early new-energy build — wrapped in a holding company that trades at a discount to the parts. On the parts our range is roughly INR 1,110–1,720 a share; the bull case (~₹1,719) needs the Jio IPO to crystallise a full digital multiple and O2C margins to hold, while the cautious case (~₹1,112) is a wider holdco discount with O2C margins compressing. What moves it is the Jio listing, the O2C margin cycle, and how wide the market keeps the holding-company discount.

Where Reliance sits in its markets

Reliance competes across several very different arenas — telecom/digital, organised retail, oil-to-chemicals, upstream oil & gas, media and new energy — and its position differs sharply in each. Rivals trade in different currencies and aren't directly value-comparable, so this is a competitive map, not a price table.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
