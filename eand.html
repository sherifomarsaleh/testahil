What the chart says

In plain terms: e& trades about 15% below our weighted central fair value of AED 22.72, and the gap is not really about next quarter’s numbers — it is the market pricing an unresolved policy decision. The UAE charges e& a federal royalty on domestic profit (38% today, deductible against a 9% corporate tax) and that regime expires 31-Dec-2026 with no replacement announced; each 4 points of royalty is worth roughly AED 1.1 a share in our DCF. Layer on a same-day event: on 10-Jul-2026 e& agreed to sell its entire Vodafone stake for AED 21.8bn gross, crystallising a passive holding into cash right as the royalty question opens up. The DCF plus a sum-of-the-parts bridge (core cash-generative business, plus the Vodafone and Mobily stakes, minus debt and minority claims) says AED 28.38; the dividend a holder actually receives today (~4.8% yield) prices at just AED 17.03 — the spread between those two is the conglomerate discount in one number. Relative multiples (23.72) and normalized earnings (18.90) sit in between. The bull case (~AED 28–29) is a favourable royalty reset plus the Vodafone cash redeployed into dividends; the bear case (~AED 17–18) is an adverse royalty widening plus a stalled deal. What moves it near-term is the CBUAE/Fed rate path, du’s competitive intensity in the UAE, and the royalty announcement whenever it lands.

Peer set & risks

Where e& sits in its markets

e& is the UAE’s incumbent telecom operator — roughly 60% of a two-player domestic market — wrapped around a conglomerate of international stakes (Maroc Telecom, PPF/e& CEE, e& Egypt, PTCL) and equity holdings (Mobily, and until 10-Jul-2026 also Vodafone). It trades at a thinner multiple than the pure domestic peer because of that NCI-heavy structure. This is a competitive map, not a price table.

How to read the odds bar

It's a picture of where the price could be — not a single guess. Here's each part:

The thick teal band is where the price lands about half the time. The two numbers above it are the edges of that "middle half."
The thin pale band is almost the whole range — about 9 times out of 10 the price stays inside it. The far numbers are the rare low and rare high.
The upright line is the middle outcome — half the simulations land above it, half below.
The gold dot is the latest price. If most of the bar sits to its right, the odds lean up; to its left, they lean down.

The sentence above the bar says the same thing in words. These are model estimates from 50,000 simulations — useful odds, never guarantees.
